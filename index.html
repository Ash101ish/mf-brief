Worth a Look

Editor's note · One pick

Axis Nifty Capital Markets Index Fund

The most interesting NFO this week, and not for the obvious reason. The fund tracks the Nifty Capital Markets TRI - a basket of exchanges, depositories, registrars, brokers, and AMCs themselves. It's an explicit bet on the infrastructure of Indian financialisation rather than on any specific market direction.

The underlying thesis: Indian household financial-asset allocation is still 5-6% in equities versus 50%+ in real estate and gold. As that share moves toward the global norm over the next decade, the entities that process the flows - not the stocks that benefit from them - capture the most predictable economics. Exchanges earn per-transaction. Depositories earn per-account. AMCs earn on AUM growth regardless of which fund wins. It's the "picks and shovels" framing applied to the equity culture story.

NFO already closed; available post-listing through normal SIPs and lumpsum. Expense ratio for an index fund will be at the lower end (likely 25-35 bps).

Skip if: your portfolio is already mid-cap heavy. Capital markets companies skew mid-cap by weight, which would compound your existing exposure to Motilal Oswal Midcap, Nippon Small Cap, and Bandhan Small Cap.

Regulatory Backdrop

SEBI's revised Mutual Fund Regulations 2026 took effect April 1, reshaping the framework most investors will quietly benefit from over the next year:

Lower TERs across categories. Restructured slabs reduce expense ratios on equity funds by roughly 5-15 bps depending on AUM tier - meaningful compounding over 10+ year horizons.

Performance-fee plans permitted. AMCs can now offer schemes where the fee scales with returns above a benchmark. Optional for now; watch for whether your AMCs introduce them and on what terms.

50% portfolio overlap cap. No thematic or sectoral fund in the same AMC can share more than half its portfolio with another scheme. If breached, AMC must rebalance in 30 days; failing that, investors can exit without exit load.

Enhanced governance and disclosure norms - more granular AMC-level risk reporting and trustee accountability.
