Worth a Look

Editorial pick · Category innovation

Zerodha Life Cycle Fund (2036 / 2041)

These are among the first products in the Life Cycle Fund category that SEBI's 2026 regulations created - open-ended equity funds with a target year that run a glide path, starting equity-heavy and rotating automatically toward safer assets as the date approaches. It is the passive-investing answer to the "rotate it yourself" problem most long-horizon investors never actually get around to.

Why surface it: your book is almost entirely active, high-conviction equity with no built-in de-risking mechanism. A dated glide-path fund is the structural opposite - it does the age-appropriate rebalancing for you, at index-fund cost, and sidesteps the manager-transition risk sitting on parts of your portfolio. Worth understanding as a concept even if you never buy, because the category is likely to expand quickly.

Skip if you prefer to control your own equity-to-debt glide manually, dislike committing to a fixed target year, or want a track record before you invest - these are brand-new funds with no history, and the glide path only helps if your real horizon actually matches the target date.
